" AN EVALUATION OF THE PHILIPPINE COAST GUARD COMPREHENSIVE STRATEGY ON MARITIME SECURITY: TOWARDS AN ENHANCED MARITIME SECURITY PLAN IN THE WEST PHILIPPINE SEA"





Ramil E. Montemar

( Researcher )




Abstract

    This research endeavor is centered around a comprehensive examination and assessment of a strategic framework aimed at bolstering efficient maritime security. The investigation is carried out by delving into the perspectives held by respondents on pivotal metrics that encompass national security, ecological wellbeing, the utilization of finite energy resources, and safeguarding physical integrity. To achieve this, the study methodically gathered pertinent data, encompassing various dimensions of the respondents' profiles, including but not limited to gender, age, the pinnacle of educational accomplishment, and the duration of their service. The intent behind this data collection was to gain a nuanced understanding of the diverse demographic makeup of those participating in the study. The outcomes of this rigorous investigation unveiled a predominantly affirmative assessment of the comprehensive strategy, signifying its efficacy in adeptly addressing a spectrum of security apprehensions. However, these positive evaluations were not evenly distributed across all subgroups. Notable disparities surfaced in the evaluations, particularly in relation to age brackets and educational achievements. Drawing on these outcomes, several actionable recommendations can be delineated. Firstly, it is advised that the strategy be tailored to cater to the distinctive needs and viewpoints of varying age clusters. This responsive approach acknowledges the different generational perspectives on security matters and aligns the strategy more closely with the values and concerns of each age group. Furthermore, the need for enhanced collaboration among different governmental bodies and agencies becomes evident. By fostering greater inter-agency coordination, the strategy's implementation can be streamlined, ensuring a more cohesive and efficient approach to maritime security management. The findings also emphasize the significance of incorporating sustainability principles and community participation into the strategy. Recognizing the interdependence between environmental conservation and maritime security, the strategy can be refined to encompass eco-friendly practices, thereby fortifying natural security. Moreover, community engagement can create a network of vigilant stakeholders who actively contribute to the safety and well-being of maritime domains. In sum, these insightful findings hold substantial implications for the strategic evolution of maritime security policies and initiatives. The research not only underscores the overarching success of the existing strategy but also elucidates avenues for further enhancement. By embracing the suggested recommendations and accommodating the diverse viewpoints of various demographics, stakeholders can bolster the resilience and effectiveness of maritime security measures in a rapidly evolving global landscape. Keywords: comprehensive strategy, maritime security, evaluation, demographic analysis, national security, natural security, non-renewable energy, physical security.
